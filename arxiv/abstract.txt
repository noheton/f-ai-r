The FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management were drafted
for data, then extended to research software (FAIR4RS) and machine-
learning models (FAIR4ML). Neither extension yet covers the artefact
classes that LLM-assisted scholarly writing produces: exportable
conversation transcripts, versioned prompt files, tool-and-model
version manifests, verification-status ladders, and per-claim
provenance maps. We propose F(AI)^2R, read F-A-I-A-I-R: the canonical
FAIR axes with an (AI) factor multiplied through them, squared because
each axis demands two passes over every artefact -- an authoring pass
and an audit pass -- and because the factor unpacks into a 2x2 of
writer x reader. The contribution is the integration of eight
individually unoriginal practices into a single discipline enforced by
a ten-stage agent pipeline with strict separation between prose-owning
and audit-owning agents: transcript preservation, verification-status
labelling, per-claim provenance maps, mirror discipline, recursive
meta-process documentation, base-rate-anchored disclosure, legal
honesty about authorship, and FAIR alignment as precondition. The
paper is its own case study: the manuscript, the prompts, the
provenance graph (PROV-O over Turtle), the logbook, the slide decks,
and the conference poster are all in the public repository, and the
cooperation that produced them is narrated and chronicled. The
pattern is offered for community contestation; some standard with
these properties is becoming load-bearing for the next decade of
scholarship under volume pressure from LLM-assisted submission.
