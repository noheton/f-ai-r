## Caption (one sentence)
The (AI)\textsuperscript{2} factor unpacked as a 2x2 grid of
writer (columns) and reader (rows): the four cells are classical
scholarship, LLM-assisted writing (the paper's primary case),
AI-assisted reading (auditor / scrutinizer / search agent), and the
AI-to-AI corpus (training data, agent-to-agent review).

## Why TikZ (not matplotlib)
The figure is argument-shape, not data-bound: four cells of fixed
content with axis labels, a hairline divider, and a right-edge
disclosure-surface band. TikZ keeps the typography in lock-step
with the surrounding LaTeX (sizing, colour tokens from the DLR
palette as defined in `paper/style/fair2r.sty`) and avoids the
font-substitution drift matplotlib introduces when rendering long
multiline cell text inside a vector PDF.

## Data / relations the figure must show
- Two axis labels: writer (top, columns split human / AI) and
  reader (left, rows split human / AI).
- Four cells with the canonical names (classical scholarship,
  LLM-assisted writing, AI-assisted reading, AI-to-AI corpus),
  each carrying a one-line subtitle (the case it covers) and a
  one-sentence note (the artefact-bundle implication).
- Cell styling that doubles colour with line style: the
  human-writer/human-reader cell is solid-green, the AI-writer/
  human-reader cell is solid-blue, the human-writer/AI-reader cell
  is dashed-blue on white, the AI-writer/AI-reader cell is
  dotted-grey on grey. Greyscale-legible: the line style alone
  identifies the cell.
- A right-edge disclosure-surface label tying the diagonal to the
  artefact-bundle widening from top-left to bottom-right.
- A footer band restating the central claim of the figure in one
  italic sentence.

dependency: the four cells are distinguished by fill + line style
+ caption position rather than by hatching.
