## Caption (one sentence)
Horizontal bar chart of human-author contributions to the
manuscript by type, read from `doc/user-contributions.md`, grouped
by family (structural / directive / corrective / meta / uptake)
and ordered by leverage with responsibility-uptake at the bottom.

## Why matplotlib (not TikZ)
The figure is data-bound: counts come from regex parsing of
`doc/user-contributions.md` and will shift on every new entry.
matplotlib lets the script regenerate on every build with no
hand-encoding of counts; TikZ would re-encode the data on each
revision and drift on the next session.

## Data / relations the figure must show
- One bar per contribution type, in the fixed order
  `structural-decision`, `rule-shape`, `content-prompt`,
  `corrective-intervention`, `observation`, `experience-meta`,
  `responsibility-uptake` (heaviest leverage at top).
- Numeric badge inside the bar if it has visual room, else just
  to its right.
- Family annotation on the right edge of each bar
  (`structural`, `directive`, `corrective`, `meta`, `uptake`).
- A title strip at the top reporting the total count and the
  source file.
- Two information channels paired: fill colour (DLR blue / yellow
  / green family) and hatch (`//` on
  `corrective-intervention` and `experience-meta` so the figure
  remains greyscale-legible).

bottom because it is the leg of the contribution that the AI
structurally cannot share.
