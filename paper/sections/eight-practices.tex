Each practice below is individually unoriginal. The argument is that
adopting any one in isolation is insufficient against the failure
modes catalogued in \S\ref{sec:failure-modes}, while adopting all
eight, with the pipeline of \S\ref{sec:pipeline} enforcing the seams,
is sufficient against most and honest about the rest.

\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.2em,itemsep=2pt]
    \item[1. Transcript preservation.]
        Every working session that touches the manuscript exports its
        conversation transcript and commits it under
        \texttt{doc/transcripts/}. Transcripts are bound to the
        manuscript via \texttt{prov:used} on the corresponding
        authoring activity, typed as \texttt{fair2r:Transcript} in
        the provenance graph.
    \item[2. Verification-status labelling
        (\S\ref{sec:ladder}).]
        Every claim carries one of the five FSM rungs. The rung is
        recorded in the graph, not in the prose; a claim invoked at a
        rung lower than its evidence permits is allowed, a claim
        invoked higher is a defect surfaced by the Aligner.
    \item[3. Per-claim provenance maps.]
        Every named claim has a \texttt{fair2r:Claim} IRI in
        \texttt{doc/provenance.ttl} with at minimum
        \texttt{prov:wasGeneratedBy}, \texttt{prov:wasAttributedTo},
        \texttt{prov:wasDerivedFrom}, and
        \texttt{fair2r:verificationState}. A retraction is a
        \texttt{prov:Invalidation} activity, never a deletion.
    \item[4. Mirror discipline (\S\ref{sec:handback}).]
        Every primary artefact has a (source, render) pair under
        version control. The build either agrees or fails.
        \emph{Asset honesty over asset completeness}: a placeholder
        is allowed and encouraged, but it must visibly identify
        itself as AI-authored and pending.
    \item[5. Recursive meta-process documentation
        (\S\ref{sec:evolution}).]
        A paper that prescribes a discipline must have been written
        under that discipline. This paper is its own case study ---
        the manuscript, the agent prompts, the provenance graph, the
        logbook, and the build pipeline are all in the public
        repository, and the cooperation that produced them is
        narrated in \S\ref{sec:evolution} and chronicled in
        Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-evolution}.
    \item[6. Base-rate-anchored AI disclosure.]
        Disclosure is anchored to numeric base rates for the failure
        modes that the rungs are supposed to mitigate. For
        fabricated citations specifically, we cite the figures that
        motivated the rung's
        existence~\cite{walters2023fabrication,magesh2024legal} and
        declare the fraction of claims at each rung at submission
        time.
    \item[7. Legal honesty about authorship.]
        AI is acknowledged but is not an author. The position holds
        across Germany (\S2 \emph{UrhG}), the United States, and the
        major medical journals~\cite{icmje2023}: AI cannot hold
        copyright, cannot consent to publication, cannot be held
        accountable. The \texttt{CITATION.cff} lists humans only;
        the graph attributes activities to AI agents but never
        promotes them to authors.
    \item[8. FAIR alignment as precondition.]
        \emph{Do not retrofit FAIR.} The eight practices, adopted on
        day one, supply the four FAIR axes at low marginal cost.
        Adopted at submission time, the cost dominates: the
        manuscript has to be re-decomposed into claims, transcripts
        reconstructed if not preserved, rungs assigned in retrospect
        with the verification overhead now maximally expensive.
\end{description}

\noindent The integration is the contribution. None of the eight is
new on its own.
