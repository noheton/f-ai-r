\section{Process Evolution During Cooperative Writing}
\label{sec:evolution}

This section is a candid record of how the F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R
pipeline itself changed while this paper was being written. The
manuscript was produced under the same pipeline it documents (the
fifth integrated practice, \S\ref{sec:eight}, item~5); the goal here
is transparency about how the paper came to be and how researcher
and AI interacted. The record is reconstructed from
\texttt{doc/logbook.md} and \texttt{doc/provenance.ttl}.

\paragraph{Summary of evolutions.}
Table~\ref{tab:evolutions} synthesises the changes the cooperation
imposed on its own scaffolding while the paper was being written.
Five classes of evolution show up: \emph{rules} (binding constraints
added to one or more agent prompts), \emph{agents} (a new specialised
agent added to \texttt{agents/}), \emph{schema} (a class or property
added to \texttt{doc/provenance.ttl}), \emph{pipeline} (a build,
deployment, or rendering change), and \emph{manuscript} (a structural
reframing of the paper itself). The full chronology lives in
\texttt{doc/logbook.md}; the table below is a deduplicated digest of
the structurally-significant evolutions and is auditable against the
forty-eight typed entries in \texttt{doc/user-contributions.md}.

\paragraph{Chronology.}
The per-step chronology of the evolutions in
Table~\ref{tab:evolutions} ---
which prompt triggered each row, what the change unlocked
downstream, and why the cooperation imposed it on its own
scaffolding rather than working around it --- is recorded in
Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-evolution}. Three threads from that
chronology are load-bearing for the position taken in
\S\ref{sec:position} and are summarised here.

\paragraph{Researcher-as-corrector.}
The model's first drafts are confidently wrong about specific
things --- DOIs, attributions, occasional reasoning chains. The
correction work is the researcher's, and the volume of it across
this paper's history is itself an argument for the verification
ladder. Without the rungs, those corrections would not have a
canonical home; with them, each one lands as a state transition on
a named claim and is auditable. The cooperation does not eliminate
the need for a careful reader; it makes the reader's contribution
legible.

\paragraph{Pipeline as target, not yet as practice.}
The ten-stage pipeline of \S\ref{sec:pipeline} is enforced on this
manuscript by the discipline of the human author rather than by
software. There is no orchestrator binary that refuses to dispatch
a writer agent until the source-analyzer has confirmed the
sources, no aligner that fails the build on a primary-artefact
desync. The shape of the pipeline is real; its automation is
aspirational. Recording the gap is the honest thing to do.

\paragraph{Logbook as conscience.}
The logbook is the place every session writes its diary. Once it
exists and is mandatory, the cooperation's drift becomes
detectable: a session that produces no logbook entry produced no
consequential work, by definition. The logbook is also where
tomorrow's reader looks first when something on the live page
disagrees with the prose --- it is the index back into the
provenance graph, written in the only language a tired researcher
can read at 2\,a.m.
