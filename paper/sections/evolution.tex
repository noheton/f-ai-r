\section{Process Evolution During Cooperative Writing}
\label{sec:evolution}

This section records how the F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R pipeline
itself changed while the paper was being written --- the fifth
integrated practice (\S\ref{sec:eight}, item~5) made visible. The
record is reconstructed from \texttt{doc/logbook.md} and
\texttt{doc/provenance.ttl}; Table~\ref{tab:evolutions} is a
deduplicated digest covering five classes (rules, agents, schema,
pipeline, manuscript), auditable against the forty-eight typed
entries in \texttt{doc/user-contributions.md}. The per-step
chronology lives in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-evolution}.

\paragraph{Three threads.}
Three threads from the chronology
(Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-evolution}) are load-bearing for the
position of \S\ref{sec:position}.
\emph{Researcher-as-corrector}: the volume of correction work
(wrong DOIs, claim--evidence drift, attribution slips) is itself
an argument for the verification ladder, which gives every
correction a canonical home as a state transition.
\emph{Pipeline-as-target-not-yet-as-practice}: the ten-stage
pipeline of \S\ref{sec:pipeline} is enforced on this manuscript
by the human author's discipline rather than by software; the
gap is recorded rather than papered over.
\emph{Logbook-as-conscience}: a session that produces no logbook
entry produced no consequential work, by definition.
