\section{Process Evolution During Cooperative Writing}
\label{sec:evolution}

\S\ref{sec:self} attests that this paper was produced by the
F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R pipeline. This section is the second half of
that attestation: a candid record of how the process itself changed
while the paper was being written. The goal is transparency. The
prescription in \S\ref{sec:pattern} is the destination; the path was
not straight, and the path is worth preserving for two reasons.
First, it shows which practices survived first contact with a real
manuscript. Second, it shows where the human researcher had to
intervene to keep the AI from drifting.

The record below is reconstructed from \texttt{doc/logbook.md} and
\texttt{doc/provenance.ttl}. Every event names the trigger (a
researcher decision, a defect surfaced by review, or a model
behaviour observed in conversation), the artefact altered, and ---
where applicable --- the failure mode of \S\ref{sec:failure-modes}
that the change now mitigates.

\subsection{Initial bootstrap}
\label{sec:evo-bootstrap}

\textbf{Trigger.} Researcher request: initialise a repository for the
paper, with prompts under \texttt{agents/}, a logbook under
\texttt{doc/}, and a single PROV-O graph that follows the creation of
the paper. Distil best practices from a prior repository
(\texttt{noheton/Obscurity-Is-Dead}) and exclude the prior repository's
domain content.

\textbf{Response.} The AI surveyed the source repository
(via a separate research subagent, to keep the surveying read-only and
out of the main writing context), produced a distillation, and
proposed an initial directory layout. The researcher accepted it.

\textbf{Artefact.} 37 files in the first commit: \texttt{paper/}
skeleton, ten prompt files, six \texttt{doc/} documents, the seed
provenance graph, machine-readable metadata at the root, dual
licensing.

\textbf{Observation.} The first AI proposal of F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R
read the ``squared'' as \emph{AI participates twice in the loop}.
Reading the prior repository carefully revealed the researcher's
original framing: \emph{(AI)} as a factor multiplied through every FAIR
axis, squared because of the two passes per axis. The two readings are
compatible but the second is the load-bearing one. The paper draft
adopts it. The lesson: when the AI's first-pass interpretation differs
from the researcher's prior writing, the prior writing wins.

\subsection{Primary-artefact consistency invariant}
\label{sec:evo-invariant}

\textbf{Trigger.} The researcher asserted, after the bootstrap commit,
that the manuscript, the PROV-O graph, and the logbook are primary
artefacts and \emph{must be consistent and up to date at all times}.
The trigger was not a defect; it was a rule the researcher knew was
needed, observed was missing, and made explicit.

\textbf{Response.} The AI added a uniform ``Primary-artefact
consistency (binding)'' block to every prompt under \texttt{agents/};
named the Orchestrator the ultimate custodian of the invariant and the
Provenance Curator its keystone; specified that the FAIR Aligner will
surface a desync as an audit \emph{fail}. The rule was also recorded
as a \texttt{fair2r:Claim} in the graph, attributed to the researcher,
at \textsc{lit-read} from session conversation; and as a
\texttt{prov:Activity} (\texttt{act:rev-consistency-rule}) revising
each affected prompt entity.

\textbf{Artefact.} A second commit, narrowly scoped: 15 files
modified, 201 insertions.

\textbf{Failure mode addressed.} Claim--evidence drift
(\S\ref{sec:fm-drift}) and silent retraction. The invariant binds
prose changes to graph changes to logbook changes; a writer that
modifies one without the others now violates a load-bearing rule
rather than a stylistic preference.

\subsection{Site, Pages workflow, paper-build CI}
\label{sec:evo-ci}

\textbf{Trigger.} Researcher request: a public site in the spirit of
the prior repository, plus continuous integration for the paper
build.

\textbf{Response.} The AI added a static-site generator
(\texttt{scripts/build\_provenance\_site.py}, parsing
\texttt{doc/provenance.ttl} via \texttt{rdflib}), the corresponding
GitHub Pages workflow with a Turtle-validation gate, and a paper-build
workflow that compiles both manuscripts on every push and uploads the
PDFs as artefacts. The site replicates the topology of the prior
repository's site; the workflow is the conventional pair of
\texttt{actions/configure-pages} and \texttt{actions/deploy-pages}.

\textbf{Observation.} The researcher noted that the prior repository
used a consent gate before deployment. We ported the structure of the
gate (Pages deploys only on \texttt{main}; the working branch sees
artefacts only) and deferred the explicit consent record to a later
commit, recorded in the logbook as a known gap. Asset honesty over
asset completeness (\S\ref{sec:practice-mirror}) was applied to the
gap itself.

\textbf{Failure mode addressed.} Mirror drift between source and
render (\S\ref{sec:practice-mirror}). The CI is the audit pass for
the build: any commit that breaks the build, or that produces a
provenance graph that fails Turtle validation, fails the workflow
visibly.

\subsection{Chapter-granularity rule for the Scientific Writer}
\label{sec:evo-chapter}

\textbf{Trigger.} While the AI was drafting the paper sections in a
single conversation, the researcher requested an explicit rule that
\emph{each \texttt{.tex} file should represent one chapter, to keep
the files at a manageable size}.

\textbf{Response.} The Scientific Writer prompt
(\texttt{agents/scientific-writer.md}) gained a binding ``File
granularity'' block: one \texttt{\textbackslash section\{\}} per file
under \texttt{paper/sections/}, soft target 100--400 lines per file,
filename slug matches the \texttt{\textbackslash label\{sec:\dots\}}.
\texttt{paper/main.tex} is now a thin assembler --- preamble plus
\texttt{\textbackslash input\{sections/...\}} in reading order ---
and prose lives in the section files.

\textbf{Failure mode addressed.} List-of-lists prose
(\S\ref{sec:fm-listoflists}) and the implicit pressure on the writer
to flatten an argument when a single file grows past comprehensibility.
A 400-line file is small enough to keep in a single review pass; a
2000-line file is not.

\subsection{Patterns observed in the cooperation}
\label{sec:evo-patterns}

Three patterns recur across the events above and are worth naming
explicitly.

\paragraph{Researcher-as-corrector.}
The researcher's interventions are short, specific, and rule-shaped:
\emph{the three artefacts must stay consistent}; \emph{tex files
should be chapter-sized}; \emph{add a section that says X}. None of
them is a rewrite of AI-produced prose. The cooperation lives in the
gap between a model that produces a plausible default and a researcher
who can recognise when the default is wrong. The pipeline of
\S\ref{sec:pipeline} is intended to make that gap shorter, not to
remove the researcher.

\paragraph{Pipeline as target, not yet as practice.}
The ten-stage pipeline is the architecture the prompts encode. The
present draft was produced by a single AI agent in conversation with
the researcher, with role separation documented in the prompts but
not yet actively switched per turn. The honest reading: this paper
encodes the pattern; the next paper, written from the same prompts
under a stricter Orchestrator that enforces single-stage handoffs,
will exercise it. We name this gap rather than paper over it.

\paragraph{Logbook as conscience.}
Every event in this section corresponds to an entry in
\texttt{doc/logbook.md}. The append-only discipline made the
reconstruction cheap: there was no need to interview the AI about
what it ``remembered'' having done, because the logbook had already
recorded it. \emph{The logbook is the conscience of the cooperation};
the provenance graph is its record.
