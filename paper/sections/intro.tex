\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data
management~\cite{wilkinson2016fair} were drafted for data, then
extended to research software (FAIR4RS~\cite{chuehong2022fair4rs}) and
to machine-learning models (FAIR4ML~\cite{ravi2024fair4ml}). Neither
extension yet covers the artefact classes that LLM-assisted scholarly
writing produces: exportable conversation transcripts, versioned
prompt files, tool-and-model version manifests, verification-status
ladders, structured redaction policies, and per-claim provenance maps.

This is not a hypothetical gap. Anyone now writing a paper with an
LLM in the room is producing those artefacts, whether or not they
preserve them. If they vanish, the audit trail goes with them ---
a reader cannot ask \emph{who wrote this sentence, what evidence
supports this claim, and how was the claim
verified?}~\cite{krebs2026obscurity} --- and the work sits exposed
to the documented failure modes of LLM-assisted writing on top of
an already-poor reproducibility
baseline~\cite{ioannidis2005,pineau2021reproducibility}\todo[inline]{verify}:
fabricated citations, claim--evidence drift, sloppification,
hedging chains. Reported base rates are
order-of-magnitude-disqualifying: 18--55\% fabrication for
general-purpose
generation~\cite{walters2023fabrication}, 17--34\% hallucination
for retrieval-augmented legal-research
tools~\cite{magesh2024legal}.

The volume problem compounds it. The fraction of journal and arXiv
submissions containing LLM-assisted prose has risen sharply since
2023~\cite{liang2024mapping,vannoorden2023chatgpt,kobak2024delving};
editors have asked for provenance \emph{from} the authors instead
of post-hoc detection \emph{against}
them~\cite{else2023chatgpt,thorp2023chatgpt}\todo[inline]{verify},
and detection alone is, on present evidence, an arms-race the
reviewer side does not
win~\cite{sadasivan2023reliably}\todo[inline]{verify}. The pattern
this paper proposes is meant to be the authoring-side complement.

\paragraph{The journal as distribution channel may be in decline.}
The strain is not only on per-paper review; it is on the format
itself. Preprint servers, overlay journals, registered reports,
and artefact-versioned repositories now cover much of what
journals historically
did~\cite{eisen2018preprints,tennant2016open}\todo[inline]{verify},
and the arrival of LLM-generated submissions on top of an already
strained system~\cite{conroy2023sleuths}\todo[inline]{verify} ---
amid venue-policy
churn~\cite{aclrr_llm_policy,iclr_llm_policy,neurips_llm_policy}
--- tips a slow drift into a visible crisis. We do not claim the
journal is dead; we claim its default-ness can no longer be taken
for granted, and that an authoring-side discipline whose primary
outputs are the \emph{repository, the provenance graph, and the
manuscript bundled together} fits the emerging ecosystem as well as
the legacy one. F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R is designed to fit both.

Since~\textcite{vaswani2017attention} put scaled dot-product attention
at the centre of sequence modelling, the population of plausible
authors of a scientific paragraph has changed in kind, not just in
degree; FAIR's 2016 vintage predates that shift by a year, and its
operational vocabulary still assumes that the agent at the keyboard
is a person.

We propose \emph{F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R}, read
\emph{F-A-I-A-I-R}: the canonical FAIR axes with an \emph{(AI)}
factor multiplied through them, squared because each axis demands
two passes --- authoring and audit. An earlier \emph{FAIR4AI}
draft was discarded as \emph{4AI heißt alles}: a suffix denotes
nothing about how the principles change. Folding \emph{(AI)} in
asserts the extension is internal to FAIR rather than external.

\paragraph{Contribution.} The contribution is not the name. It is the
integration of eight individually unoriginal practices into a single
discipline: (1) transcript preservation; (2) verification-status
labelling; (3) per-claim provenance maps; (4) mirror discipline
between source and render; (5) recursive meta-process documentation;
(6) base-rate-anchored AI disclosure; (7) legal honesty about
authorship; and (8) FAIR alignment treated as a precondition, never
as a retrofit. None of the eight is novel on its own. Their joint
adoption, enforced by a ten-stage agent pipeline with strict
separation between prose-owning and audit-owning agents, is what we
claim is new. \emph{The pipeline is the team; the team is the
methodology.}
