\section{Provenance Analysis}
\label{sec:provenance-analysis}

The graph at \texttt{doc/provenance.ttl} is not a passive record. It
is a queryable structure whose health, taken section by section,
\emph{is} the audit pass on \textbf{Reusable} (\S\ref{sec:axes}).

\begin{figure}[!h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.96\linewidth]{figures/provenance-topology.pdf}
\caption{Shape of the F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R provenance graph,
read from the live \texttt{doc/provenance.ttl} at compile time.
Five PROV-O lanes (Agents, Plans, Activities, Entities, Claims)
carry the backbone of the schema; the satellite band underneath
carries the Contribution mirror that records who --- human or AI
--- did what (\S\ref{sec:authors-note}). Each node is decorated
with its instance count; the figure regenerates from the same
graph the queries below run against, so a future audit pass that
shifts the cardinalities shifts this figure too.}
\label{fig:provenance-topology}
\end{figure}

The audit reads four diagnostic SPARQL queries against the live
graph: rung distribution (per-claim verification state),
section-to-claim coverage (sections present in prose vs.\ in the
graph), source invocation strength (citations actually invoked by a
claim, surfacing ``ghost'' citations), and the activity timeline
(the dated record of substantive change). The queries, their results
on the present manuscript, and the auto-generated tables they emit
into the prose at compile time are walked in
Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-provenance-analysis}.

\begin{figure}[!h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.96\linewidth]{figures/ladder-populations.pdf}
\caption{Verification ladder with live rung populations. Each node's
area is proportional to the count of \texttt{fair2r:Claim} entities
currently at that rung in \texttt{doc/provenance.ttl}; the topology
is the same FSM as Figure~\ref{fig:ladder-fsm} but the figure now
also tells the reader where the manuscript stands. The dotted
vertical rule marks the model ceiling: rungs left of it are
deliverable by an LLM agent alone, rungs right of it require a
human reading the cited literature. The dotted yellow back-edge is
the only legal back-edge --- a retraction recorded as a
\texttt{prov:Invalidation}, never as a deletion. Regenerated on
every build by \texttt{paper/figures/src/ladder-populations.py}.}
\label{fig:ladder-populations}
\end{figure}

A submission whose load-bearing claims sit below
\textsc{lit-read} fails the FAIR audit unless the manuscript hedges
explicitly --- the rung distribution
(Figure~\ref{fig:ladder-populations}) tells the reviewer where
hedging is mandatory and where it would be over-cautious.

\paragraph{What the analysis is and is not.}
The four queries are diagnostic, not exhaustive. They surface the
failure modes of \S\ref{sec:failure-modes} that have a graph
signature: ghost citations, drift between prose and graph, sections
that have not yet been curated. They do not surface
\textbf{semantic} drift --- a claim that has been correctly recorded
in the graph but whose phrasing in the prose has shifted away from
its evidence. That is the readability scrutinizer's job
(\S\ref{sec:pipeline}), not the graph's. A live, more interactive
view of the same graph is published at
\href{https://noheton.github.io/f-ai-r/provenance.html}{the
\textbf{Provenance} tab of the public site}, with a click-to-inspect
node browser and the same SPARQL queries available client-side.
