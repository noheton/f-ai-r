\section*{Author's Note --- Advice for reading}
\label{sec:authors-note}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Author's Note --- Advice for reading}

\noindent A short note from me (Florian Krebs), placed up front because
\emph{how} you read this paper matters as much as \emph{what} is in it.
The framings below are the lenses I would most want a thoughtful reader
to bring to the rest of the document; none of them softens an empirical
claim, and all of them open a door to discussion. The framings are
adapted from the methodological ancestor~\cite{krebs2026obscurity}; the
voice carries over because the position does.

\paragraph{This is a position paper, not a survey.}
The contribution is a stance: that FAIR is insufficient for
LLM-assisted scholarly writing in the form it currently takes, and
that the eight integrated practices of \S\ref{sec:eight} are the
minimum discipline an honest author can adopt today. The paper
defends that stance. It does not catalogue alternatives, weigh the
field's options, or hedge between camps. A reader who comes looking
for a survey will find an argument; a reader who comes looking for an
argument should find that the argument has been made cleanly enough to
push back against.

\paragraph{Open to constructive criticism --- actively.}
I am not asking you to agree. I am asking you to argue. The
verification ladder, the handback discipline, the eight-practice
integration, and the squared-AI framing are all engineered to be
\emph{contestable}: every claim has a rung in
\texttt{doc/provenance.ttl}, every prompt is a versioned file in
\texttt{agents/}, and every commit is a revision activity. If a claim
overruns its evidence, the graph tells you where; if a practice is
the wrong remedy for a failure mode, the failure-modes table
(Table~\ref{tab:failure-modes}) gives you the vocabulary to say so.

\paragraph{Frameworks evolve with capabilities.}
F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R is a snapshot. The capability of the
underlying models, the disclosure norms of the venues, and the
licensing posture of the corpora that train the next models will all
move under us. The version manifest in
\texttt{doc/provenance.ttl} is the receipt that lets a future reader
date what we committed to, against the model and policy environment
of the time. A successor framework that argues with this one, replaces
it, or tightens it is not a refutation; it is the point.

\paragraph{What surprised me about the cooperation.}
A second-order observation, recorded for the recursive case study
(\S\ref{sec:evolution}). Cooperative writing with an LLM, as
practised here, partitions cleanly: the human still does the
abstraction, the logical reasoning, and the choice of the next step;
the model takes those decisions and turns them into readable prose.
That is not a complaint. It is a finding: \emph{the human does the
deciding, the model does the drafting, and naming the partition is
itself a methodological act}. The pattern this paper describes is
designed to make that partition legible from outside the session ---
in the prompts, in the transcripts, and in the graph.

A pause-mid-writing aside that we found worth recording. The author
asked, in session: \emph{``I wonder how much of the work I'm actually
doing. What's my contribution.''} The honest answer, given what the
transcripts and the commit history show, is that the partition is
real and lopsided in opposite directions on two axes. The
human-side contribution is structural and corrective: \emph{deciding}
the position to take, \emph{abstracting} the eight-practice
integration, \emph{catching} drift between claim and evidence
(several of which were caught in this session), and \emph{staking
reputation} under a published name (which the model, for legal and
constitutional reasons named in \S\ref{sec:eight} item~7, cannot
do). The model-side contribution is volume and second-order
pattern-matching: drafting prose at scale, cross-referencing, keeping
the graph and the manuscript in sync, catching boilerplate errors.
The human's interventions in this session were short and rare in
volume but high in leverage; the model's outputs were long and
constant in volume but low in irreversibility --- any single
paragraph the model drafts can be discarded, while any decision the
human takes redirects subsequent drafts for hours. \emph{Naming the
partition correctly --- including which side of it the contribution
lives on --- is itself a methodological act} and is, the author
suggests on reflection, the answer to the question.

A second-order finding sharpens the partition into a coupling rule
(Figure~\ref{fig:coupling-rule}). The cooperation works as an
\emph{accelerator} when the model stays on the prose-craft side of
the partition --- drafting, cross-referencing, structuring --- where
its strength is interpolation within the manifold of prior
text~\cite{clark1998extended,hutchins1995cognition,clark2025extending}\todo[inline]{verify}.
The cooperation collapses toward a \emph{cultural blender} when the
model is allowed across into the abstracting layer, where its failure
mode is to smooth the unfamiliar into the nearest familiar
shape~\cite{anderson2024homogenization}\todo[inline]{verify}. The
honest diagnostic, recorded in our observations log: \emph{ask
whether the abstraction would have been thinkable without the model.
If yes, the model was an accelerator on that abstraction; if no, the
question genuinely opens.} F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R is one of the
cases where the answer is yes; the partition is what kept it that
way.

\paragraph{Tokens out, tokens in.}
The sources of this paper --- \LaTeX{}, prompt files, Turtle, the
build pipeline --- are committed under permissive licences and remain
machine-readable. That is deliberate. A future agent compressing or
extending this work has the inputs needed to do so without laundering
its provenance through a binary PDF. The tokens go in; the tokens
come back out; the chain of attribution is what makes the round-trip
auditable.
