\section{Limits, dual-use, and standing objections}
\label{sec:limits-and-objections}

The pattern is not free. We owe the reader an explicit account of
what F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R consumes, what it depends on, and
what the standing objections to it look like.

\paragraph{Resource cost and frontier-model dependence.}
Every authoring and audit pass burns compute, and the verification
ladder amplifies it: a contested claim is read by an agent, a
human, sometimes a second agent, and the FAIR aligner. The energy
and water footprint of frontier-model inference is paid by someone,
somewhere~\cite{strubell2019energy,patterson2021carbon,luccioni2024power,li2023thirsty}\todo[inline]{verify}.
We extend practice~6 (\S\ref{sec:eight}): disclosure should
\emph{include the order-of-magnitude inference budget consumed by
the manuscript}, declared at submission time alongside the rung
distribution. Model behaviour also drifts as models
change~\cite{chen2023drift}\todo[inline]{verify}; the transcripts
of practice~1 and an explicit \texttt{fair2r:modelId} on every
\texttt{fair2r:Activity} are partial mitigation, giving a
deprecated agent a known headstone.

\paragraph{Equity.}
Groups without frontier-model access cannot apply this pattern at
the same effective rung; the floor for ``\textsc{ai-confirmed}
understands the source'' is set by the state of the
art. F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R \emph{describes} the work; it does
not \emph{equalise} access to
it~\cite{bender2021stochastic,birhane2022values}\todo[inline]{verify}.
Two partial mitigations: smaller-model fallbacks for routine
audit passes (layout, hedging-density, list-of-lists detection);
and the structural property that an honestly-declared lower-rung
distribution is preferable to a forged higher-rung one, with the
ladder making the difference legible to the reviewer.

\paragraph{Adjacent proposals and adjacent vocabularies.}
The concurrent \emph{Agent-Native Research Artifact} (Ara) of
\textcite{liu2026ara} replaces the narrative paper with a
four-layer package (logic, executable code, exploration graph of
failed branches, claim-evidence grounding) and reports
reproduction-success and QA-accuracy gains on PaperBench and
RE-Bench. F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R declines that bargain: we
keep the human-readable manuscript as the primary artefact and
ride a PROV-O graph alongside it. Ara's exploration layer is a
strict superset of our logbook plus claim-graph and indicates a
credible upper bound for what the same substrate could carry.
F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R is also sometimes mistaken for a
dataset-metadata proposal; the dataset-metadata slot is already
occupied by MLCommons Croissant~1.0
\cite{benjelloun2024croissant}. Croissant describes the
\emph{thing learned from}, F(AI)\textsuperscript{2}R the
\emph{authoring process}; the two compose --- a Croissant record
can be referenced from a \texttt{fair2r:Source} triple --- but
neither subsumes the other.

\paragraph{Standing objections.}
\emph{``Just FAIR with extra steps.''} Two prior re-readings
(FAIR4RS~\cite{chuehong2022fair4rs},
FAIR4ML~\cite{ravi2024fair4ml}) judged that adding sub-principles
for new artefact classes was preferable to absorbing them; the
audit pass on \textbf{Reusable} is the load-bearing case ---
replaying a writing pipeline is materially different from replaying
a build.
\emph{``Eight practices on day one is too expensive.''} Eight is
the \emph{minimum}: Table~\ref{tab:failure-modes} shows dropping
any one exposes a specific failure mode the others do not cover,
and the \textsc{ai-confirmed} rung was introduced exactly to
attack the verification-overhead bottleneck (\S\ref{sec:ladder}).
\emph{``Provenance graphs do not prevent fraud.''} Correct; the
graph aims to make fraud \emph{detectable by inspection} ---
graph-prose contradictions, claims referencing non-existent
sources, \texttt{prov:wasGeneratedBy} without a transcript are all
checkable defects, in contrast to unstructured AI-assisted writing
where the same fraud leaves no artefact a third party can point
at.
\emph{``You cannot check the model's reasoning, only its
output.''} Yes; the discipline makes the \emph{output's
relationship to the input} checkable, even when the model's
internals are not --- that is what the ladder, the Source
Analyzer hand-off, and the human-reader rung are for.
\emph{``A position paper should not also be a worked example.''}
Practice~5 requires that a paper prescribing a discipline have
been written under it; the recursive case study is how the
position can be checked.
