# 1. Motivation

---

# FAIR for data, FAIR4RS for software, FAIR4ML for models

The community has re-read FAIR for **data** (Wilkinson 2016),
**software** (FAIR4RS, Chue Hong 2022) and **models**
(FAIR4ML, Castro & Garijo 2024).

**LLM-assisted scholarly writing produces a fourth artefact class** none of those re-readings covers:

- exportable conversation transcripts
- versioned prompt files
- tool-and-model version manifests
- verification-status ladders
- structured redaction policies
- per-claim provenance maps

A third FAIR re-reading is required.

---

# The base rates are not anecdotal

- Walters & Wilder (2023): **55 % fabrication for GPT-3.5, 18 % for GPT-4** in general-purpose citation generation.
- Magesh et al. (2024): **17–34 % hallucination** in retrieval-augmented legal-research tools.
- Liang et al. (2024): rising fractions of LLM-modified prose across major venues.
- Kobak et al. (2024): excess-vocabulary detection picks up the same signal.
- Sadasivan et al. (2023): **AI-text detection is, on present evidence, an arms race the reviewer side does not win.**

The audit trail must be built **in** by the author, not bolted **on** by the reviewer.

---

# Reproducibility was already the baseline

- Ioannidis 2005 (PLOS Medicine): "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False".
- Pineau et al. 2021 (JMLR): NeurIPS reproducibility report — discipline-specific evidence.
- The LLM failure modes **sit on top of** a baseline that was already poor.
- ML venues (NeurIPS, ICLR, ACL ARR) have published explicit author-and-reviewer AI policies. They cover *whether* to disclose; they do not yet cover *what to preserve*.

# 2. Position

---

# Five numbered claims

1. **FAIR is insufficient** for LLM-assisted scholarly writing in its current form.
2. The required extension is **multiplicative, not appended** — *4AI heißt alles*.
3. **Eight integrated practices** are the minimum discipline.
4. The pipeline is the team; **specialisation reduces hallucination at the seam.**
5. Naming the practice is the first step toward **contesting it.**

> The position-paper genre: prescribe; defend; anticipate objections; invite second-case-study replication.

# 3. The F(AI)²R pattern

---

# Axes: F(AI)²R

| | F | A | I | R |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AI₁ Authoring** | DOI, ORCID, IRIs | clone-and-build, licences | Turtle graph, fair2r: ns | prompts, Makefile, logbook |
| **AI₂ Audit** | metadata agree, IRIs unique | sources vendored | Turtle validates, SHACL | clone replays, drift reported |

The (AI) factor multiplied through every axis. **Squared** because each axis demands two passes — authoring and audit.

---

# Ten-stage pipeline (handback-disciplined)

```
0. Orchestrator → 1. Research → 1.5. Source Analyzer → 2. Scientific Writer → 3. Illustration
                                                                                       ↓
                                  ┌────────── handback (dashed) ─────────────────────────┐
                                  ↓                                                      │
8. Site Agent ← 7. Provenance Curator ← 6. Aligner ← 5. Readability ← 4. Layout Scrutinizer
```

- **Authoring stages (blue)** own prose; never mark their own claims verified.
- **Audit stages (green)** never edit prose; file defect registries; the writer repairs.
- **Curation stage (yellow)** alone writes `doc/provenance.ttl`.

---

# Verification ladder as a finite-state machine

`unverified-external` → `needs-research` → `lit-retrieved` → **`ai-confirmed`** → **`lit-read`**

- `lit-retrieved`: identifier resolves; abstract read by an AI agent.
- `ai-confirmed`: AI agent has read the source and confirmed the inference (with quoted snippet).
- **`lit-read`: a human author has read the source itself.** Required for load-bearing claims at submission.
- Crosswalk: `lit-retrieved`/`ai-confirmed`/`lit-read` ↔ PRISMA Identification/Screening/Included; GRADE certainty floor: low / moderate.
- Retraction is `prov:Invalidation`, never deletion.

---

# Handback and mirror disciplines

- **Handback.** Scrutinizers never edit source. They write defect registries under `doc/handbacks/`. The repair is a separate commit by the prose owner. *What did the scrutinizer actually flag?* has a single canonical answer in the git history.
- **Mirror.** Every primary artefact has a (source, render) pair. The build either agrees or fails.
- **The invariant.** *The manuscript, the PROV-O graph, and the logbook are primary artefacts and must remain consistent and up to date at all times.* Slide decks join them as the fourth.

# 4. Eight integrated practices

---

# Eight practices, none novel on its own

| # | Practice | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Transcript preservation | session loss |
| 2 | Verification-status labelling (FSM) | claim–evidence drift |
| 3 | Per-claim provenance maps | fabricated citations |
| 4 | Mirror discipline | placeholder dishonesty |
| 5 | Recursive meta-process documentation | methodological-tract failure |
| 6 | Base-rate-anchored AI disclosure | perfunctory disclosure |
| 7 | Legal honesty about authorship | inflated co-author claims |
| 8 | FAIR alignment as **precondition** | retrofit cost |

**The integration is the contribution.**

---

# Why eight (and not seven, or nine)

- Each individual practice mitigates one or more named failure modes.
- Removing any one **exposes a specific failure mode that the others do not cover**.
- Adding a ninth (e.g. the *supervisory-glance* / multi-tracked-research pattern) is on the table for revision.

The bibliography for each is grounded in real literature: stochastic parrots, datasheets, model cards, PRISMA, GRADE, ICMJE, USCO, UrhG, fabrication base rates, model collapse, AI-energy literature.

# 5. Domain ontologies and the longer arc

---

# Extensibility through domain ontologies

PROV-O is the spine; it does not have to be the only ontology in the graph.

- **SOSA / SSN** (Janowicz 2019) for sensor-observation claims.
- **OM-2** (Rijgersberg 2013) and **QUDT** for units of measure.
- A `fair2r:Claim` carries `prov:wasDerivedFrom` for **evidence** and a domain-ontology link for **value semantics**.

Failure modes prose alone cannot reach (a value coherent in its citation but **wrong in its units**) become **graph queries** and SHACL shapes. *Konkrete standardisierte Methode.*

---

# The bioinformatics precedent

Under data-volume pressure, bioinformatics **standardised semantic tagging** (the Gene Ontology, Ashburner 2000) so that genes, proteins, and their relations could be referenced unambiguously across labs.

- The ontology was **not a research output**; it was the precondition for research that scaled.
- The community's investment in tagging is what made the next decade possible.

LLM-assisted scholarship is now in an analogous moment.

---

# Future research success: three properties

| Property | F(AI)²R support |
|---|---|
| **Provenance models** that turn published claims into machine-readable hypotheses | verification ladder; per-claim graph |
| **Graph interconnectivity** across institutional knowledge graphs (Helmholtz cross-centre, fully-instrumented) | PROV-O + domain ontologies |
| **Clever experimental validation** that uses the graph to identify the next testable deviation | recursive case study; audit pass |

Some standard with these properties is now load-bearing for the next decade.
F(AI)²R is one offer among several the community can refine.

# Field notes from the second cooperative writing project

- **Researcher role shifts toward experimentation.** Empirical disciplines win; paper-driven disciplines (philosophy) face an uncomfortable question.
- **Prompt-craft is closer to programming than to drafting.** Familiar loop for CS-trained researchers.
- **Responsibility includes the rooms after publication.** Conferences, panels, peer correspondence — the AI cannot share that load.
- **Cooperation as coupling rule.** The model accelerates when it stays on the prose-craft side; it becomes a cultural blender if allowed across into the abstracting layer.
- **Diagnostic.** *Would the abstraction have been thinkable without the model?*

# 7. Failure modes and sustainability

---

# Eight named failure modes (and three residual)

| Mode | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Fabricated citations | verification ladder + provenance map |
| Claim–evidence drift | handback discipline |
| Sloppification | mirror discipline on quotations |
| Model collapse | transcript preservation + disclosure |
| List-of-lists prose | readability scrutinizer |
| Hedging chains | hedge-density rule (1 per 6–8 sentences, paired) |
| Placeholder dishonesty | asset honesty over completeness |
| Dual use | made visible, not eliminated |

Residual: pre-publication leakage, audience fatigue, adversarial review.

---

# Sustainability is not a footnote

- Inference burns compute (Strubell, Patterson, Luccioni, Li). **Disclosure should include the order-of-magnitude inference budget alongside the rung distribution.**
- Frontier-model dependence is a **stranded-asset risk**. Receipts are preserved (transcripts, prompts, version manifest); capability is not portable.
- Equity: groups without frontier-model access cannot apply F(AI)²R at the same rung. Smaller-model fallbacks for routine audit passes; honest declaration of lower-rung distribution is preferable to a forged higher-rung distribution.

# 8. Anticipated objections

---

# Six objections we have heard

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "Just FAIR with extra steps." | Re-readings are precedent; the audit pass on **R** is materially different. |
| "Eight practices is too expensive." | Eight is the minimum; dropping any one exposes a failure mode. |
| "Graphs add bureaucracy without preventing fraud." | Graphs make fraud **detectable by inspection.** |
| "You cannot check the model's reasoning." | True; we check the **output's relationship to the input.** |
| "This privileges well-resourced groups." | Real; named honestly; partial mitigations. |
| "Position paper should not also be worked example." | Practice 5 requires the recursion. |

# 9. Submission conditions and call to action

---

# Submission conditions (RDA / FORCE11 / HMC)

We will submit F(AI)²R to RDA and FORCE11 as a candidate community recommendation **when**:

1. Human-author consent recorded in `doc/publication-consent.md` (currently *NOT YET GIVEN*).
2. **At least one external case study** has adopted the pattern on a paper unrelated to either the source repository or this one, public under a permissive licence.

The conditions are the minimum we need to distinguish a single-author idiosyncrasy from a transferable pattern.

---

# The longer arc

F(AI)²R is **the entry ticket, not the destination.**

We are not arguing F(AI)²R has to be **the** standard.

We are arguing that **some standard with these three properties** —
provenance models for hypothesis development,
graph interconnectivity across institutional knowledge graphs,
clever experimental validation against the graph —
is now load-bearing for the next decade of scholarship.
